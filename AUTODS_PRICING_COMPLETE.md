## üìä **PRODUCT COST RANGE - PROFIT RULES**

### **Recommended Setup:**

**Rule 1: Low-Cost Items ($0 - $20)**
```
$0 - $20: 50% profit
```
**Why 50%?**
- Higher risk on cheap items
- Need bigger margins to cover fees
- Example: $10 cost ‚Üí $15 sale ‚Üí $5 profit

**Rule 2: Mid-Range Items ($20 - $100)**
```
$20 - $100: 40% profit
```
**Why 40%?**
- Sweet spot for most products
- Good balance of profit and competitiveness
- Example: $50 cost ‚Üí $70 sale ‚Üí $20 profit

**Rule 3: High-Value Items ($100 - $500)**
```
$100 - $500: 35% profit
```
**Why 35%?**
- Lower margin but higher dollar profit
- More competitive pricing
- Example: $200 cost ‚Üí $270 sale ‚Üí $70 profit

**Rule 4: Premium Items ($500+)**
```
$500 - $99999: 30% profit
```
**Why 30%?**
- High-dollar items need competitive pricing
- Still huge profit in absolute terms
- Example: $1000 cost ‚Üí $1300 sale ‚Üí $300 profit

---

### **How to Set It Up in AutoDS:**

**Click "Add Rule" or "Enter a lower max price" to create multiple rules:**

```
Rule 1:
Product Cost Range: $0 - $20
Profit %: 50%
Profit $: $0.99

Rule 2:
Product Cost Range: $20 - $100
Profit %: 40%
Profit $: $0.99

Rule 3:
Product Cost Range: $100 - $500
Profit %: 35%
Profit $: $0.99

Rule 4:
Product Cost Range: $500 - $99999
Profit %: 30%
Profit $: $0.99
```

**This creates a tiered pricing strategy:**
- Cheap items = Higher margin (compensate for risk)
- Expensive items = Lower margin (but bigger dollar profit)

### **Set Price Cents Value:**

```
Set Price Cents Value: .99
```

**Options:**
- `.99` = Always end in .99 ($14.99, $29.99)
- `.95` = Always end in .95 ($14.95, $29.95)
- `.00` = Round numbers ($15.00, $30.00)

**Recommendation: `.99`**
- **Psychology:** $14.99 feels cheaper than $15.00
- **More sales:** Customers see $14.99 as better deal
- **Industry standard:** Most stores use .99

**Examples:**
- Without .99: $15.00 ‚Üí Customer thinks "$15"
- With .99: $14.99 ‚Üí Customer thinks "$14" (even though it's $15!)
